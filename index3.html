Present Tense

Simple Present Tenses

Definition:
Used to express habits, general truths, repeated actions, and fixed events.

Formula

(+) Subject + V1 (base form) / V1+s/es (for he/she/it)
(–) Subject + do/does + not + V1
(?) Do/Does + Subject + V1?
Examples:

She walks to school every day.
They do not eat meat
Does he play football?

Present Continous Tense

Definition:
Used to describe actions happening right now or temporary situations. Also used for planned near-future actions.

Formula

(+) Subject + am/is/are + V-ing
(–) Subject + am/is/are + not + V-ing
(?) Am/Is/Are + Subject + V-ing?
Examples:

I am studying right now.
She is not sleeping.
Are you watching TV?

Present Perfect Tense

Definition:
Used to express actions that happened at an unspecified time in the past and are still relevant or have results in the present.

Formula

(+) Subject + have/has + V3 (past participle)
(–) Subject + have/has + not + V3
(?) Have/Has + Subject + V3?
Examples:

I have finished my homework.
He has not eaten breakfast.
Have they arrived?

Present Perfect Continuous Tense

Definition:
Used to express an action that started in the past and is still continuing now, focusing on duration.

Formula

(+) Subject + have/has + been + V-ing
(–) Subject + have/has + not + been + V-ing
(?) Have/Has + Subject + been + V-ing?
Examples:

She has been studying for two hours.
I have not been feeling well.
Have you been working all day?
