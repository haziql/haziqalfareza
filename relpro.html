Relative Pronoun

Definition

Relative pronouns connect clauses and provide more information about a noun. They introduce relative clauses.

Usage Examples:

WHO (for people - subject/object)
Example:
- The man who lives next door is a doctor. (subject)
- The woman who I met yesterday is my teacher. (object)
WHOM (for people - object, formal)
Example:
- The person whom you called has arrived.
- The student to whom I gave the book is absent.
WHOSE (showing possession)
Example:
- The girl whose car was stolen reported to police.
- I know a man whose brother is famous.
WHICH (for things/animals)
Example:
- The book which I bought is interesting.
- The dog which barked all night belongs to my neighbor.
THAT (for people or things)
Example:
- The movie that we watched was boring.
- The teacher that taught us was very kind.
WHERE (for places)
Example:
- This is the house where I was born.
- The school where I studied has been renovated.
WHEN (for time)
Example:
- I remember the day when we first met.
- Summer is the season when people go on vacation
WHY (for reasons)
Example:
- I don't understand the reason why he left.
- That's why I couldn't come to the party.
