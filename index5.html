Future Tenses

Simple Future Tense

Definition:
Used to express future actions, decisions made at the moment, or predictions.

Formula

(+) Subject + will + V1
(–) Subject + will not (won’t) + V1
(?) Will + Subject + V1?
Examples:

I will call you later.
She won’t come today.
Will they join the meeting?

Future Continous Tense

Definition:
Used to describe an action that will be in progress at a specific time in the future.

Formula

(+) Subject + will + be + V-ing
(–) Subject + will not + be + V-ing
(?) Will + Subject + be + V-ing?
Examples:

This time tomorrow, I will be traveling.
He won’t be working tonight.
Will you be staying with us?

Future Perfect Tense

Definition:
Used to describe an action that will be completed before a certain point in the future.

Formula

(+) Subject + will + have + V3
(–) Subject + will not + have + V3
(?) Will + Subject + have + V3?
Examples:

By next week, I will have finished this book.
She won’t have arrived by 8 PM.
Will they have completed the task?

Future Perfect Continuous Tense

Definition:
Used to describe an ongoing action that will continue up to a specific point in the future, focusing on duration

Formula

(+) Subject + will + have been + V-ing
(–) Subject + will not + have been + V-ing
(?) Will + Subject + have been + V-ing?
Examples:

By next month, he will have been working here for five years.
I won’t have been waiting long.
Will you have been studying all day?
