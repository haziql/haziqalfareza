Past Tense

Simple Past Tense

Definition:
Used to describe *completed actions that happened at a specific time in the past

Formula

(+) Subject + V2 (past form)
(–) Subject + did not + V1
(?) Did + Subject + V1?
Examples:

He visited Paris last summer.
They did not finish the project.
Did you see that movie?

Past Continuous Tense

Definition:
Used to describe actions that were in progress at a certain time in the past, or when another action interrupted it.

Formula

(+) Subject + was/were + V-ing
(–) Subject + was/were + not + V-ing
(?) Was/Were + Subject + V-ing?
Examples:

I was watching TV when you called.
They were not sleeping.
Was she cooking dinner?

Past Perfect Tense

Definition:
Used to describe an action that happened before another action or time in the past.

Formula

(+) Subject + had + V3
(–) Subject + had + not + V3
(?) Had + Subject + V3?
Examples:

She had left before I arrived.
We had not finished the test.
Had they already eaten?

Past Perfect Continuous Tense

Definition:
Used to describe a continuous action that was happening before another action or time in the past, often emphasizing duration

Formula

(+) Subject + had + been + V-ing
(–) Subject + had + not + been + V-ing
(?) Had + Subject + been + V-ing?
Examples:

They had been studying for three hours before the power went out.
I had not been feeling well.
Had she been working there long?
