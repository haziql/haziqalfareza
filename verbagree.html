Subject-Verb Agreement

Definition

Subject-Verb Agreement is a grammatical rule that requires the subject and verb in a sentence to agree in number (singular or plural). If the subject is singular, the verb must be singular. If the subject is plural, the verb must be plural.

Basic Rules

- Subjek tunggal + verb bentuk tunggal (verb + s/es)
- Subjek jamak + verb bentuk dasar (tanpa s)
- “I” dan “You” menggunakan verb dasar meskipun tunggal

Example

Singular Subject + Singular Verb
Examples:
- She writes a letter every day.
- The cat sleeps on the sofa.
- John plays football.
Plural Subject + Plural Verb
Examples:
- They write letters every day.
- The cats sleep on the sofa.
- John and Mary play football.
